# Labor Market Impacts of Paid Family Leave: Econometric Analysis (Python)

## Overview
This project analyzes the long-term labor market effects of California's Paid Family Leave (CA-PFL) policy using econometric methods and microdata from the Current Population Survey (CPS). The analysis evaluates how the policy influenced labor force participation, employment, unemployment duration, and earnings across demographic groups.

The goal of the project is to estimate causal effects of the policy using Difference-in-Differences and Triple-Difference econometric models.

## Research Question
What are the long-term labor market impacts of California's Paid Family Leave policy on labor force participation, employment, unemployment duration, and earnings, and how do these effects differ across demographic and education groups?

## Data
The research uses individual-level data from the Current Population Survey (CPS), specifically the Annual Social and Economic Supplement (ASEC) and the Basic Monthly CPS files, covering the years 1996 to 2016.

This 20-year period allows the analysis to observe labor market trends before and after the implementation of California's Paid Family Leave law in 2004.

## Methods
- Across-State Difference-in-Differences (DDA)
- Within-State Difference-in-Differences (DDW)
- Triple Differences (DDD)

## Summary
Results suggest limited overall labor market impacts, with modest changes in employment outcomes across demographic groups.
